TRACKER GUIDE

STRAIT OF HORMUZ - MARITIME INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM - v3

WHAT IS THIS TRACKER?

This tracker monitors maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, one of the world's most critical energy chokepoints. Roughly one-fifth of global oil trade transits this corridor.

It covers three connected basins: the Persian Gulf, the Strait narrows, and the Gulf of Oman. It tracks vessel flow, speed changes, anchorage buildup, AIS-off events, and dark-transit reappearance patterns.

DATA MODE

STATUS LABELS

SIMULATION: Route-based traffic simulation is active.

CONNECTING: Live AIS websocket handshake in progress.

LIVE: Real AIS messages are flowing and updating positions.

FALLBACK: Live feed unavailable or stale; simulation remains active automatically.

THE THREE ZONES

PERSIAN GULF

Upstream loading basin. Tankers here are often loading or preparing outbound transit toward the Strait.

STRAIT NARROWS

The high-sensitivity chokepoint. Traffic compression, speed drops, and AIS interruptions are most important here.

GULF OF OMAN

Downstream basin. Vessels here typically completed Strait transit and continue toward the Indian Ocean or return inbound.

VESSEL TYPES

VESSEL COLOURS & TYPES

Cyan - Tanker / LNG carrier (strategic energy cargo).

Green - Cargo / container vessels.

Red - Naval / military.

Amber - Unknown type from available AIS fields.

Orange - Dark vessel (AIS disabled or unavailable).

INTERACTING WITH VESSELS

Hover a vessel for name/type/speed/zone/destination. Click map icon or list row to pin full detail in the right panel.

Underway vessels are directional arrows; anchored vessels are circular markers.

DARK VESSELS

WHAT A DARK EVENT MEANS

A dark vessel is one that stops broadcasting track updates. In this tracker, the vessel disappears from normal map rendering, DARK AIS count rises, and a dark alert is added to the feed.

Dark behavior can be technical or operational. In a chokepoint context, repeated dark events during transit are treated as high-priority anomalies.

DARK-TRANSIT TRACKING UPGRADE

THREE-PHASE TRACKING MODEL

Phase 1 - Visible: vessel is tracked with live/sim position, heading, and speed.

Phase 2 - Blind: AIS goes dark near approach lanes; vessel movement continues on hidden transit routing.

Phase 3 - Reacquired: vessel reappears in the opposite basin and is highlighted as a completed dark transit.

PERSIAN GULF -> GULF OF OMAN

Outbound vessel goes dark near Gulf-side approaches, remains blind through Strait corridor, then reappears in Oman-side waters with transit labeling.

GULF OF OMAN -> PERSIAN GULF

Inbound vessel goes dark near Oman-side approaches, remains blind through narrows, then reappears in Gulf waters with route and gap context.

VISUAL CUES FOR DARK TRANSITS

During dark phase: a last-seen orange marker remains on map.

On reappearance: vessel gets cyan highlight + pulse, list row gets REAPPEARED badge, and a dashed bridge line links last-seen to reacquired position.

Detail panel includes DARK ROUTE, LAST AIS POS, REAPPEARED, and estimated AIS GAP.

TOP BAR METRICS

VESSELS / TANKERS / AVG KN / DARK AIS

VESSELS: tracked vessels in theater.

TANKERS: tanker/LNG subset.

AVG KN: average speed in knots.

DARK AIS: active dark vessels.

STATUS BADGE

NOMINAL: traffic profile near baseline.

ELEVATED: anomaly present (speed/flow/anchorage/dark mix).

ALERT: severe disruption conditions.

DARK VESSEL: one or more active dark events.

LEFT PANEL - FLOW

Inbound -> Gulf, Outbound -> Sea, Slow/Anchored, and Dark/No AIS bars show real-time split of operating conditions.

Sparkline shows vessel count trend versus baseline, useful for rapid disruption/recovery assessment.

RIGHT PANEL - ANALYTICS

Chokepoint Risk Index combines dark-rate, slowdown, anchored share, and flow-loss signals.

Oil & LNG Flow estimates throughput from active outbound energy vessels and speed factor.

Stranded & Waiting highlights anchorage and low-speed delay pressure.

Alert Feed streams event-level context including dark-transit complete events.

Interpretation note: Simulation mode is route-constrained and behavior-based, not a full hydrographic/nav-aid engine. Live mode reflects actual AIS messages when available.
