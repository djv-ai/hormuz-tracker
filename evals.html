Every metric displayed in the Hormuz tracker has a defined calculation. This reference shows the exact formula, data source, and known limitations for each — use it to build eval tasks that test whether LLMs correctly attribute claims to evidence.

RISK INDEX
Chokepoint Risk Index
CALC
Formula: (dark_rate×40 + slow_rate×20 + flow_drop×30 + anchored_rate×10) × 100
Where dark_rate = dark_vessels / total, slow_rate = slow_vessels / total, flow_drop = max(0, 1 − oil_MBD/17), anchored_rate = anchored / total.
Weights: Dark vessels most heavily weighted (40%) reflecting OPEC-analyst practice of treating AIS loss as primary anomaly signal. Flow drop (30%) reflects economic significance.
Limitation: Composite score; a single factor spiking can dominate. Not calibrated against historical events.
Congestion Score
AIS
Source: AIS speed-over-ground (SOG). HIGH = slow_vessels > 3 · MOD = 1–3 · LOW = 0.
Slow = underway vessel with SOG < 6 kn. Normal strait transit speed is 11–15 kn.
Limitation: SOG threshold is heuristic. Gulf of Oman monsoon (May–Sep) naturally reduces speeds to 7–9 kn.
Dark AIS Rate
AIS
Source: dark_count / total_vessels × 100%. A vessel is "dark" when its AIS transponder stops broadcasting while underway (SOG > 2 kn was last known state).
Limitation: AIS gap ≠ deliberate evasion. Technical fault, military OPSEC, and piracy avoidance are alternative explanations. Cannot confirm intent from AIS alone.
OIL & LNG FLOW
Outbound Crude (MBD)
EST
Formula: outbound_tankers × 2,000,000 bbl × speed_factor / 1,000,000
Where speed_factor = avg_speed / 13 kn (capped at 1.0). Baseline assumption: VLCC avg capacity 2M barrels. Normal baseline: ~17 MBD (IEA).
Limitation: Assumes all outbound tankers are loaded (may be in ballast). Actual cargo not confirmed from AIS. DWT is self-reported and can be falsified.
Outbound LNG (BCF/day)
EST
Formula: LNG_carriers_outbound × 3.4 BCF/day × speed_factor
LNG carriers identified by vessel name prefix "LNG" or ship-type code 80–89 where DWT < 180,000. Baseline: ~1.8 BCF/day (GIIGNL annual report).
Limitation: LNG identification by name is imprecise. A fully loaded Q-flex carries ~3.4 BCF; smaller vessels less. Not adjusted for boil-off.
World Oil Share (%)
CALC
Formula: (oil_MBD / 17) × 20%
Assumes Hormuz carries ~20% of global oil supply at the 17 MBD baseline (IEA / EIA Hormuz factsheet). Scales linearly with estimated outbound flow.
Limitation: Global supply denominator (~100 MBD) is static. Does not account for SPR releases or non-Hormuz supply changes.
STRANDED CARGO
Anchored Count
AIS
Source: AIS SOG < 1 kn or AIS navigational status = "AT ANCHOR" (status code 1). Includes all vessel types in tracked region.
Limitation: Vessels may anchor legitimately for loading/discharging operations — elevated anchored count is not always a distress signal.
Stranded Cargo Value ($B)
EST
Formula: (anchored + slow_vessels) × $180M avg cargo value
$180M is a blended average across tankers (oil cargo) and cargo vessels (manufactured goods, grain). VLCC crude cargo alone = ~$120–160M at $60–80/bbl.
Limitation: Highly sensitive to oil price. Does not distinguish loaded from ballast vessels. Order-of-magnitude estimate only.
WHAT CANNOT BE CONCLUDED FROM AIS
HARD LIMITS — LLMs commonly hallucinate past these boundaries
Cargo manifest — AIS reports vessel type and size, not what is onboard. A tanker may be in ballast.
Dark vessel intent — Technical fault, military OPSEC, and piracy avoidance are alternatives to sanction evasion.
Price impact magnitude — AIS flow data does not contain inventory levels, spare capacity, or demand elasticity.
Speed drop cause — Gulf of Oman monsoon (May–Sep) naturally reduces SOG. Threat vs weather requires external data.
Destination accuracy — AIS destination is self-reported and frequently falsified on sanctioned routes.
Oil grade / quality — Vessel DWT gives capacity; actual grade (WTI, Brent, Oman, Iranian Heavy) is not in AIS.
